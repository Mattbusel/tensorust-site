How it works, and how it could be wrong

What it does

Your query is resolved to a company or organization, then matched against public government datasets: federal contract awards (USAspending / FPDS), SEC-reported financials from 10-K filings (EDGAR), federal lobbying disclosures (Senate LDA), research grants (NIH RePORTER), FDA drug approvals (openFDA), and known-exploited vulnerabilities (CISA KEV). The receipt reports what those records show, links every figure, and states a conservative verdict.

The verdicts

  • Verified - a specific record supports the claim.
  • Partially supported - the claim fits the totals but not as stated (for example, a total across many awards rather than one contract).
  • Needs context - the record differs in a way that matters, most often an announced ceiling versus dollars actually obligated or paid.
  • Insufficient evidence - the datasets queried do not contain enough to judge. We say so rather than guess.

How it could be wrong

  • A verdict is only as good as entity resolution; a namesake or subsidiary can throw off a match.
  • Prime-contract data excludes subcontracts, classified spending, and money routed through resellers or IDV vehicles, so some totals are a lower bound.
  • Announced contract "values" are often ceilings; obligated and outlaid dollars are what is committed and paid, and they can differ by a lot.
  • This is what the public record shows, not a legal finding.
