What we build

Not research. A competitive outcome you can put in front of a buyer, a board, an agency or an investor.

Each one is a defined deliverable built from primary records, with every figure linked back to the source and the coverage limits written in.

01

Incumbent Weakness Map

Where the larger competitor is actually exposed. Contracts approaching recompete, thin or expiring patent coverage, recalls and safety findings, published security exposure, lapsed registrations, quiet retreats from a segment. Every exposure tied to the record that shows it.

Bought by founders and commercial leads who need to know where to push.
02

Market Entry Evidence Pack

What the market actually looks like before you commit to it. Who holds the contracts, which agencies and buyers are active, what the standards require, who the incumbents are, and where public money is already moving.

Bought by strategy and US expansion leads.
03

Contract Opportunity Map

Federal and institutional buying activity in your category. Who won, how much was actually obligated against the announced ceiling, when the recompete lands, and which offices buy the thing you make.

Bought by business development and capture leads.
04

Regulatory Window Monitor

The standards, rulemaking, agency programs and funding lines that open or close a window for your technology — tracked continuously, so a change reaches you while you can still act on it.

Bought by policy leads and regulated-market operators.
05

Competitor Claim Audit

Test what a competitor says in public against what the record shows. Announced contract value against dollars actually obligated. Claimed approvals against the regulatory record. Claimed scale against filings. Supported, overstated, or missing context — with the evidence attached.

Bought by product marketing and commercial teams.
06

Investor Diligence Packet

Institutional-quality diligence on a company or a sector without an institutional research operation. Assembled from primary records, with the limits stated plainly so a partner can rely on it in an investment committee.

Bought by partners and principals at smaller funds.
07

Technical Credibility Pack

The cited evidence a conservative buyer needs before adopting something new: the research base behind the technology, federal validation and funding, standards position, and comparable public deployments. Built to be handed directly to a skeptical customer.

Bought by industrial and advanced-material companies selling into cautious markets.
08

Monthly Competitive Evidence Monitor

The department that keeps running. Funding, standards, policy, contract and competitor changes in your category, delivered on a cadence, so nobody on your team loses days searching for something that moved last week.

Bought by teams who need the advantage to compound, not expire.

Method & trust

Why you can put it in front of a buyer, a board, or an agency.
  • Primary records only. Federal contracts and awards, corporate filings, lobbying disclosures, grants, patents, regulatory and safety records, and the scholarly literature — with rights and provenance retained on every record.
  • Traceable claims. Every figure links to the record it came from, so the person you hand it to can verify instead of trust.
  • Stated limits. Coverage, sampling and entity-matching caveats are written into every deliverable. A finding we cannot support is reported as unsupported.
  • No fabrication. We never invent demand, contacts, competitors, or results.
  • Judgment stays with you. Tensorust does not replace domain expertise, legal review, or technical diligence. It removes the days spent assembling the evidence those judgments rest on.
